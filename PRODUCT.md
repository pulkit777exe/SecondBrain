# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Researchers and students who need to organize and save content from multiple platforms (Twitter/X, YouTube, Instagram, Reddit, GitHub, LinkedIn, Spotify, SoundCloud, Loom) for later reference. They work in a research or academic context, gathering and curating resources over time.

## Product Purpose

A personal bookmarking application that allows users to save, organize, and revisit content from multiple platforms. Users can tag content, filter by content type, and generate shareable links for their curated collections. Success looks like users building a personal knowledge base they can actually find things in.

## Brand Personality

Minimal and refined. Clean, minimal, sophisticated — the interface fades away. The design should feel effortless and understated, letting the content take center stage. No visual noise or unnecessary decoration.

## Anti-references

No specific anti-references. Open to any aesthetic direction that maintains the minimal, refined approach.

## Design Principles

1. **Content first** — The interface recedes; saved content is the hero. Nothing competes for attention.
2. **Effortless organization** — Tags and filtering should feel natural, not like a productivity tool imposed on the user.
3. **Quiet sophistication** — Every design decision should feel considered but not showy. Refinement over novelty.
4. **Personal, not social** — This is a private tool for personal knowledge management, not a social platform. No engagement mechanics.
5. **Reliable utility** — Works consistently across content types. The app earns trust through consistency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Consider reduced motion preferences. Support keyboard navigation for all actions.
